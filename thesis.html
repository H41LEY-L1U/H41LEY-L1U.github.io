    • Cheering for Noodles: Food Metaphor in Media Transmission

    MA Thesis Overview, posted in March, 2022
    Conceptual Metaphor Media Semiotics COVID-19 Pandemic

    Since its initial outbreak in the beginning of 2020, the novel coronavirus disease (COVID-19) has been spreading across the globe in a hectic way. This highly infectious disease, recognized as a global threat, has received intensive attention and discussion on an international level as people attempt to identify, understand and navigate through this public health emergency.

    From a cognitive linguistic and semiotic perspective, studies have been conducted to investigate the dynamics of public discourse centering the pandemic, and the embodiments of people’s emotional reactions and coping strategies. However, how people conceptualize the disease in online discussion, what linguistic, social properties and semiotic relations are represented in its media transmission under the context of crisis communication have remained understudied.

    As an attempt to fill in the aforementioned gap, this research focuses on the metaphorical conceptualization of the COVID-19 pandemic, semiotic implications of such conceptualization in public discourse, and its functionality in national branding and public opinion management.

    Why were people cheering for noodles during the pandemic?

    Since the COVID-19 pandemic hit Wuhan China hard, Chinese netizens, news media outlets, and government-affiliated media have been addressing the city of Wuhan by its representative dish, sesame hot noodles, metonymically. Other provinces and cities of the country have also been depicted as their signature cuisine in this discussion.

    The development of this food metaphor in news reporting and on social media has since then taken on a life of its own and became a nationwide cultural phenomenon. This study aims to investigate the following questions: 1). How did the circulation of this food metaphor take place in the public crisis communication and why? 2). What psychological contents, semiotic activities, and sign relations can be observed from such media transmission compared to other conceptualization of the pandemic?

    What is Wuhan Hot Noodles?
    Hot dry noodles, known in Chinese as 热干面, also transliterated as dried and spicy noodles, is a traditional dish of Wuhan, the capital of Hubei province in central China.
